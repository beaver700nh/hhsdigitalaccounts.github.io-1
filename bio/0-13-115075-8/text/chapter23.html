Of some 1.5 million known species of organisms, more than two thirds are animals. And the vast majority of these animal species are invertebrates—animals without backbones. One place you can explore a sample of this rich diversity is in a rocky tide pool. Besides its colorful sea stars and sea anemones, this habitat is home to a variety of crabs, snails, and barnacles. If you looked under a ledge, you might find brightly colored tube worms and small sponges. In fact, a typical tide pool contains examples of each of the nine phyla of invertebrates discussed in this chapter. These nine groups have the greatest number of species and are the most abundant and widespread. Before you begin the chapter, go online to the WebQuest to explore life in a rocky tide pool.
