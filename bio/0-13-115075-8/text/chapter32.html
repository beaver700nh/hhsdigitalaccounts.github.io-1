The hot desert sun beats down on a bicyclist in California's Death Valley National Park. Despite the extreme conditions in the external environment, the bicyclist's internal environment remains within a certain range of conditions. The body's relatively constant internal environment compared to the outside is called homeostasis. In this chapter you will investigate the roles of the excretory system, the liver, and the endocrine system in maintaining homeostasis.
