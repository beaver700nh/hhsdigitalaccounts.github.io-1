A desert ecosystem supports a rich community of organisms, such as the curved-bill thrasher and the saguaro cactus. Even the desert soil is alive with fungi and bacteria actively at work breaking down dead organic matter. This ecosystem seems hardy in its ability to persist in the very hot and dry conditions of the American Southwest. But as in any ecosystem, its complex network of relationships is vulnerable to change. As you will read in this chapter, changes in one aspect of the ecosystem can affect the entire network. Before you begin, go online to the WebQuest to investigate how changes in one ecosystem are affecting its community of organisms.
