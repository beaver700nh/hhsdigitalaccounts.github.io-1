It might surprise you to learn that the human body contains far more bacteria than it has body cells. Some of these bacteria are helpful. Most are merely harmless. But some have the potential to make you sick and even kill you. Your body wages a constant battle against harmful microscopic organisms. For example, specialized white blood cells attack bacteria and foreign debris when they enter the body. In this chapter, you will read about the full array of defenses your body uses in fighting infections. Before you begin the chapter, go online to the WebQuest to explore some early medical pioneers' research on infections and disease prevention.
