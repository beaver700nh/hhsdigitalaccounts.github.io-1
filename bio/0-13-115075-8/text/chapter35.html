A population of hundreds of millions of monarch butterflies migrates each winter from the United States to particular forests in Mexico. As large as this monarch population is, there are limits to its growth. Each female monarch lays hundreds of eggs, and yet the world is not overrun with monarch butterflies. What factors influence the number of monarchs in a population? Does harsh weather kill many of these insects? Is population size related to food supply? How do other species of organisms affect monarch numbers? How, in turn, do monarchs affect populations of the other species in their community? Asking questions such as these is part of studying the ecology of populations and communities, the topics of this chapter.
