Have you ever heard the expression, "You are what you eat"? That doesn't mean that you would turn into a salad or a fruit cup after sampling them. But your body is made from compounds built from the raw materials in the food you eat. In addition to these building materials, food provides a supply of energy for all the work done by your cells.

In this chapter you'll read about how your body acquires the raw materials it needs from the food you eat. Before you begin, go online to the WebQuest to explore a familiar organ of the digestive system.
