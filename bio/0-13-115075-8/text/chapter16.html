Water in a hot spring is literally boiling–heated to over 100°C by thermal energy from Earth's interior. The vivid reds, oranges, and yellows that paint the rocks are microorganisms that thrive in these extreme conditions. In heat that would kill any eukaryotic cell, these prokaryotes flourish. In fact, they would die at "normal" temperatures.

Some species of prokaryotes thrive in places that are too hot, too cold, too salty, or too acidic for any eukaryote. However, the vast majority of prokaryotes live in more moderate environments. You can find them everywhere: on doorknobs, in food, on your skin, and even in your intestines. In this chapter you will read about prokaryotes that are beneficial as well as those that are harmful.
