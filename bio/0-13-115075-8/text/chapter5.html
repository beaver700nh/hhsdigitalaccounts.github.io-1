From giant lily pads in a pond to the slender tree trunks on its banks to the delicate wings of insects humming in the warm air, all life's structures share a common element: carbon. The carbon-containing molecules of life range from simple structures of just a few atoms to complex constructions of several million atoms. Some of these molecules function as sources of energy, others as tools and machinery, and still others as storage for massive amounts of information. In this chapter, you will read about how these carbon-based molecules are made and how they function. Before you begin, go online to the WebQuest to explore biological molecules in some familiar foods.
