A redwood tree can reach tens of meters in height and several meters in diameter. Where does all the mass come from to build such a huge plant? And how does the plant move the materials it needs for growth over vast distances to reach all its cells? In this chapter you'll read about the sources of a plant's nutrition as well as mechanisms for transporting these nutrients and water within the plant. Before you begin, go online to the WebQuest to learn about carnivorous, or meat-eating, plants.
