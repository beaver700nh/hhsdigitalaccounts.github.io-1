Adaptation to life on land that began with amphibians continued with the evolution of reptiles, birds, and mammals. The feathered birds and the hairy mammals such as a lionness and her cubs are the most visible and diverse land-dwelling vertebrates today. But the scaly reptiles had a much longer reign. A hundred million years ago, reptiles, including giant dinosaurs, were the most diverse and widespread tetrapods on land. In fact, ancient reptiles included the ancestors of birds and mammals, which still show signs of their reptilian roots.
