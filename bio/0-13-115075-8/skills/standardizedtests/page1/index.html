Studying for Standardized Tests

OBJECTIVE: to strategize on the best ways to prepare for test taking

Test-taking is a necessary part of your high school experience. The kinds of tests you take range from short quizzes to long, complex final exams and standardized tests such as the Scholastic Aptitude Test (the SAT). The grades you earn in your classes are largely determined by your performance on tests. Whether you are taking a standardized test in biology, English, math, or history, there are some basic strategies that you can use. In this activity, you'll learn and practice strategies that will help you approach tests with confidence and a plan for success. When you are finished, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Karrie has a 9:00 a.m. test on Saturday. On Friday night, she set her alarm for 8:15 a.m., knowing that she would have to rush to get to the test on time, but thinking that it would be best to get a little extra sleep. Is this a good strategy? Explain.

2. Jason is facing a difficult test this morning. He's feeling a little nervous and decides to skip breakfast so he can squeeze in more time studying for the tests. Is this a good strategy? Explain.

3. Benita thinks that the best way to study for her upcoming test is to wait until the night before the test so that the information will be fresh in her mind. She stays up late studying for the test. Is this a good strategy? Explain.
