Math Review

OBJECTIVE: to apply the rules of significant figures in measurement and in mathematical operations

Suppose you measure the height of a plant and get a measurement of 42 inches. You then convert this number to meters (m) by dividing 42 inches by 39.36 inches/meter. Your calculator gives the value of 1.0670731. It would be a mistake to report this as the height of the plant. The measurement should be reported as 1.1 m because reporting digits beyond the original two that you started with would be meaningless. Knowing when to round off a number involves the rules of significant figures. In this activity, you'll review the rules and practice applying them.
