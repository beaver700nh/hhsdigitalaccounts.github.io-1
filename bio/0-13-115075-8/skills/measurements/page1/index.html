Making Measurements

OBJECTIVE: to review units of measurement used in the study of biology and to practice conversions among units

Measurement plays a critical role in science. Scientists throughout the world use the International System of Measurement, abbreviated as SI (from the French name, Système International d'Unités). SI is derived from the metric system, which is based on units of ten. Each unit is ten times larger or ten times smaller than the next unit. So conversion from one metric unit to another can be as simple as moving a decimal point (each position equals one power of ten).

The metric prefixes kilo-, centi-, and milli- are useful in measuring objects you encounter in everyday life. In biology, you must sometimes use two other prefixes for extremely small measurements: micro- (one millionth) and nano- (one billionth). The micrometer (µm), for example, is one millionth of a meter and is appropriate for measuring most cells. The nanometer (nm) is one billionth of a meter and is used to measure the size of small cellular organelles and molecules.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. How are metric units defined so it is easy to convert from one metric unit to another?

2. What are the most appropriate metric units for measuring:

a. the length of a typical cell
b. the distance between two cities
c. the temperature outdoors
d. the volume of water in a test tube
e. the mass of a person

3. A small aquarium tank is 30 cm long and 20 cm high, and the water in it is 20 cm deep. Using the relationship that 1 cubic centimeter of water has a mass of 1 gram, determine the mass of water in the tank in kilograms.

4. Prefixes define the size of metric units. For each of the following prefixes, indicate how it changes the size of the base unit to which it is attached.

a. kilo-
b. centi-
c. milli-
d. micro-
e. nano-

5. You correspond with a family friend in France whom you have never seen. In describing himself he says he is 181 cm and weighs 75 kg. Convert these measurements to inches and pounds.
