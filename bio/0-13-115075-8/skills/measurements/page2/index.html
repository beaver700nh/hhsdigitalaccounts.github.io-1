Making Measurements

OBJECTIVE: to practice making precise measurements with common laboratory instruments

Whether you are using a ruler to measure length, a balance to measure mass, a graduated cylinder to measure liquid volume, or a thermometer to measure temperature, there are basic principles to follow in making good measurements. In this activity, you will practice making measurements. When you finish, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What instruments would you use the measure the following:

a. the mass of a bird egg
b. the volume of water for watering a plant

2. Explain why it is important to zero a balance before taking a mass measurement.

3. Two people read the volume of liquid in a graduated cylinder. The first person records a volume of 12.3 mL and the second person records a volume of 12.4 mL. If they are looking at the same liquid in the same cylinder, how might the difference be explained?
