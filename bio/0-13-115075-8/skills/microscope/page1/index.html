Using a Microscope

OBJECTIVE: to identify the parts of a compound light microscope

The microscope is one of the most useful tools in a biology laboratory. In a high school lab, the most common microscope used is the compound light microscope (LM). It contains a combination of lenses that focus and bend light, producing an enlarged (or magnified) image of a specimen. The lenses in a light microscope include an eyepiece lens and typically two or three other interchangeable lenses called objective lenses. The magnification of each lens is always marked on the objective. In this activity, you will explore the parts of a light microscope and learn how to use it.
