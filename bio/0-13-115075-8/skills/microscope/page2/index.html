Using a Microscope

OBJECTIVE: to practice skills needed to use the compound light microscope in the lab

Now you will learn how to use a compound light microscope in the lab. Note that the microscope in this animation has three lenses. For each step, carefully follow the directions in the blue boxes. You will then learn how to prepare a wet-mount slide. When you have finished, scroll down to answer the questions below. Before you use an actual compound light microscope in the lab, link to these important safety guidelines and read through them.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. When do you use the coarse focus adjustment knob on a microscope?

2. Suppose you are looking at protists under the microscope and cannot see anything on low power. What adjustment could you make to the microscope that might help you see the protists, without switching to a higher magnification?

3. Suppose you focused on an organism using medium power, but then cannot see the organism after switching to high power. What should you do?

4. When putting together a wet-mount slide, why is it important to make sure that there are no air bubbles under the cover slip? What should you do if there are air bubbles?
