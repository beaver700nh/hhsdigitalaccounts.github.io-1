OBJECTIVE: to investigate carbon dating

All living organisms contain carbon in two forms: the isotopes carbon-12 (C-12), which has 6 neutrons in its nucleus, and carbon-14 (C-14), which has 8 neutrons and is radioactive. When an organism dies, it no longer takes in carbon and its remaining radioactive C-14 decays into nitrogen-14 (N-14). Over time, the ratio of C-12 to C-14 in the organism's remains changes, so by measuring this ratio you can calculate the time since death. Every 5,730 years (1 half-life), 50 percent of the original amount of C-14 has decayed. After about 50,000 years (8 half-lives), a sample has lost nearly all of its C-14 and can no longer be dated using this method.
