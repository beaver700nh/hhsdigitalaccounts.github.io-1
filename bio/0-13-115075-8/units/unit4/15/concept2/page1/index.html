Evolution is usually a remodeling process.


Build a complex eye.

OBJECTIVE: to explore the eye as an example of how complex structures might evolve from simpler ones

Charles Darwin had difficulty explaining how a complex organ such as the eye might have evolved. In this activity, you will explore the varying complexity of eye structure within one group of animals, the mollusks, and examine how a more complex eye might have evolved. When you have finished, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Can a patch of light-sensitive cells form an image?

2. The "eyecup" eye of the slit shell mollusk does not form an image. How is this type of eye still useful?

3. If evolution of the eye is a "remodeling process," how has the number of light-sensitive cells changed—has it increased, decreased, or remained the same?
