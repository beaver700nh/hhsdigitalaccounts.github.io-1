Evolutionary biology is important in health science.


Analyze sickle cell genotypes.

OBJECTIVE: to explore how an allele that produces harmful effects might occur at a relatively high frequency in a population

People who inherit two copies of the sickle cell allele produce abnormal hemoglobin and suffer from sickle cell disease. The sickle cell allele (and the disease) is found in relatively high frequencies in regions of Africa and Asia where malaria is also common. Malaria, transmitted to humans by mosquitoes, is a serious disease caused by a parasite that infects the red blood cells of its host. What is the link between the high incidence of sickle cell disease and malaria? Find out in this activity. When you have finished, scroll down to answer the questions below.
