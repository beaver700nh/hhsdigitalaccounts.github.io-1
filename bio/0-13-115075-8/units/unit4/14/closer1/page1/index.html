Microevolution is a change in a population's gene pool.


Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

OBJECTIVE: to describe the Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium and calculate genotype frequencies based on Hardy-Weinberg assumptions

In 1908, two scientists named Hardy and Weinberg described how to calculate the genotype frequencies in a population from the allele frequencies. However, they made the assumption that no microevolutionary processes were occurring, and the population was very large. In this activity, you will learn how the Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium is used to predict genotype frequencies when the allele frequency is known.
