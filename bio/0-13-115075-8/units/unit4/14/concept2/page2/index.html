Evolution has left much evidence.


Analyze amino acid sequences.

OBJECTIVE: to examine similarities in amino acid sequences

You just studied how homologous structures provide evidence for evolution. Biologists also use molecular data to study the evolutionary ancestry of species. Species that have genes and proteins with very similar sequences are likely to have inherited those molecules from a common ancestor. The more alike the sequences, the more closely related the two species are likely to be. In this activity, you will compare the amino acid sequences of a protein, cytochrome c, among five different species and determine if this sequence data, and similar data from hemoglobin, reflect evolutionary relationships.
