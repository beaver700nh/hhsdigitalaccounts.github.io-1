Travel with Darwin.

OBJECTIVE: to explore evidence that led to Darwin's theory of evolution

The HMS Beagle was a British ship assigned to complete a government survey of the "southern extremity of America." During the Beagle's five-year voyage, the ship's naturalist, Charles Darwin, investigated a variety of ecosystems. When the ship arrived in a new port, Darwin explored the countryside and catalogued the animals, plants, and the geology of each new area. Years later, these observations contributed to Darwin's theory of evolution. In this activity, you will travel virtually to some of the same places visited by Darwin, and investigate one piece of evidence that helped Darwin formulate his ideas.
