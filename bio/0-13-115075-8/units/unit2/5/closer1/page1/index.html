Closer Look: Protein Structure

OBJECTIVE: to explore levels of structure in proteins

All organisms make hundreds to thousands of large molecules called proteins. Proteins have many functions, including defending against disease, forming structures in cells, carrying oxygen, and acting as catalysts (enzymes). Each protein has a unique shape that is vital to its specific function. As you will see in the activity below, proteins can have up to four levels of structure.
