Carbon is the main ingredient of organic molecules.


Examine the world of carbon in molecules.

OBJECTIVE: to discover how some organic molecules, called polymers, are built and broken apart

Many organic molecules are huge! Some of these large molecules, called polymers, are built in a cell when similar subunits link together. These subunits are called monomers. In this activity, you will see how a polymer chain is built and broken down. When you have finished, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete the answers, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Describe the role of water in the building and breaking down of polymers.

2. You just ate a big pasta dinner. Pasta contains a polymer called starch, which in turn is made up of many monomers called glucose. What do you think happens during the digestive process to break down the starch into usable glucose?
