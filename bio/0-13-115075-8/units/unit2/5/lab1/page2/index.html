Introduction
You will start your investigation of biological molecules and denim processing by exploring denim cloth and the structure of cotton—a natural fiber produced by the cotton plant. You will look at the structure of the biological molecules that make up cotton fibers and discover a method that can be used by jeans manufacturers to soften and lighten denim jeans.

Background
Denim jeans were originally designed in the 1840s to be outdoor work clothes. The fabric had to be strong enough for the work of farmers, miners, and cattle ranchers. These jeans were not soft and comfortable when new. Denim fabric is stiff, and if it is not processed, only softens after weeks of wear and many washings. Today, jeans are not only worn by outdoor workers. Most people want to buy jeans that are already comfortable and have a "worn" feel to them. Jeans manufacturers continually look for cost effective, safe methods to treat denim and make jeans attractive to consumers.
