The cell builds a diversity of products.


Transport insulin to the bloodstream.

OBJECTIVE: to understand how cellular products are transported throughout the cell and how some are released from the cell

The pancreas is an organ that produces proteins such as digestive enzymes and the hormone insulin. Insulin regulates the level of glucose in the blood of many animals, including humans. In this activity, you will help a pancreas cell move insulin molecules through the cell's system of membranes and then secrete them into the bloodstream. When you finish the activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

2. What happens to the insulin molecules after the vesicle has fused with the cell's plasma membrane?

3. What is the name of the process when vesicles fuse with plasma membranes and release their contents outside of the cell?

4. Why is exocytosis necessary for exporting molecules like insulin from the cell?
