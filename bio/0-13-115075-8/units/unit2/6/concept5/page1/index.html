Chloroplasts and mitochondria energize cells.


Magnify a chloroplast and a mitochondrion.

OBJECTIVE: to study the structures of a chloroplast and a mitochondrion

Chloroplasts trap light energy and convert it into the chemical energy of sugar in a process called photosynthesis. Mitochondria use the chemical energy stored in food and convert it into the chemical energy of adenosine triphosphate (ATP) in a process called cellular respiration.
