Membranes regulate the traffic of molecules.


Investigate movement across the membrane.

OBJECTIVE: to explore how ions and large molecules cross membranes

Cellular membranes are selectively permeable. They act as "gatekeepers" for the cell, permitting only certain molecules to pass through. Transport proteins embedded in the membrane aid larger polar molecules and ions that can't pass through the phospholipid portion of the membrane. In this activity, you will investigate transport proteins and find out how they facilitate the diffusion of ions and large polar molecules into the cell.
