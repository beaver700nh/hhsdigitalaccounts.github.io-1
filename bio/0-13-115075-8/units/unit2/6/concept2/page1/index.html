Membranes organize a cell's activities.


Dissect a plasma membrane.

OBJECTIVE: to understand the structure of a membrane

Cells and their organelles have unique selectively permeable boundaries, called membranes, which are composed primarily of phospholipids and proteins. In this activity, you will investigate the arrangements of these molecules in the membranes. When you finish, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Why is the membrane described as a phospholipid bilayer?

2. How does does the arrangement of the phospholipids form a bilayer?

3. In Chapter 4 you learned that water is a polar molecule. How does this polarity influence the way in which the phospholipids are arranged in the membrane?
