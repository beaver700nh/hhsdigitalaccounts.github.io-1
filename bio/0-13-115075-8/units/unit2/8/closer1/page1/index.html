The light reactions convert solar energy to chemical energy.


Light Reactions

OBJECTIVE: to explore how light energy is converted into chemical energy

It is important to note that the pigments (chlorophyll a and b, carotene, and xanthophyll) are not scattered randomly throughout the chloroplast but are located within the thylakoid membrane in structures called photosystems.

All of the pigments (shown as green circles) within the photosystem capture light energy and pass it to one special chlorophyll a molecule (the reaction-center chlorophyll).
