Photosynthesis uses light energy to make food.


Explore photosynthesis in action.

OBJECTIVE: to review the major structures of a leaf

The year is 2025, and gasoline supplies are dwindling. A bioengineer is trying to find an alternative source of automobile fuel. Sugar might be an excellent fuel, and green plants produce abundant sugar by photosynthesis. Therefore, you need to find out more about the process of photosynthesis in order to help the bioengineer tap this resource. You know that photosynthesis occurs in green leaves, so your first task is to pinpoint exactly where in the leaf the sugar is made. Later you will discover how it is made.

As you go through this activity, you will see this diagram at the top of each page. The diagram will help you track which part of the leaf you are investigating. As you can see, magnification of each view is also indicated.


Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. When you have completed a question, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What structure allows for rapid diffusion of gases?

2. What is the outer protective cell layer called?

3. What are the photosynthetic cells in the interior of the leaf called?

4. What is the name of the opening in the leaf that allows exchange of gases?

5. What structure transports food and water?


You have discovered where sugar is made, but you still need to help the bioengineer understand how this sugar is made.
