The light reactions convert solar energy to chemical energy.


Investigate the nature of light.

OBJECTIVE: to discover how scientists isolate plant pigments that capture energy from the sun

Sunlight is an important ingredient in a plant's ability to make sugar. How is that sunlight captured? Plant cells contain pigments, which are colored substances found in leaf cells. The pigments absorb energy from the sun and use it to create ATP, NADPH, and O2 in the light reactions. You may think that all plant pigments are green, but is this true?
