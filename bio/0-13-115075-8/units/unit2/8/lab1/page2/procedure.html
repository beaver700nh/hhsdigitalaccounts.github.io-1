Investigative Lab 8 Procedure

1. Obtain one of each type of ivy leaf: light green young leaf, or "Lighty;" and dark green old leaf, or "Forest".
2. Using a single hole punch, punch out 10 leaf disks from "Lighty".
3. Place the leaf disks you cut in a syringe barrel and label the syringe immediately.
4. Repeat Steps 1-3 for "Forest".
5. After both syringes are loaded and labeled, put in the plungers slowly, pushing down until the plunger is touching the leaf disks. Take care not to squish them.
6. Draw up about 5 cc of the bicarbonate/detergent solution into each syringe.
7. Invert each syringe and tap it to release air bubbles.
8. Push in the plungers to move all the air out of the tips of the syringes.
9. Place your finger over the tip of one syringe while pulling the plunger back to 10 cc. Be careful not to pull back too far or you will pull the plunger out! This creates a vacuum, allowing air to flow out of air spaces in the leaf disks.
10. Hold the vacuum by keeping your finger on the tip and shake the syringe several times to release the bubbles.
11. With the tip of the syringe pointing up, let go with your finger and see if the leaf disks sink. If they do, stand the syringe up in front of the light source (which is turned off). If some of the leaf disks are still floating, repeat steps 8-11 until they sink. This may take several tries. If there are one or two stubborn disks, just leave them floating.
12. After both racers are in their "starting blocks", near the light source, turn on the light and start timing. The race begins! Whenever you see a disk rise to the top, fill in the time and total number of disks up in a chart like the one below.

13. Every 2 or 3 minutes, you can rotate both syringes one turn to loosen any leaf disks that may be stuck to the sides of the syringe. When 5 of the 10 disks have floated to the top, a leaf has finished the race. Keep timing until both racers have finished.
