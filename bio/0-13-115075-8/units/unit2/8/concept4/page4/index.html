Photosynthesis has a global impact.


Interpret carbon dioxide data.

OBJECTIVE: to understand the atmospheric impact of photosynthesis

Photosynthesis indeed has a global impact. When plants in the northern temperate zones undergo photosynthesis, they represent a huge "sink" for atmospheric CO2, as you can see in the equation for photosynthesis:

6CO2 +6H2O C6H12O6 + 6O2

The valleys in the monthly graphs correspond to summertime in the Northern Hemisphere, when active terrestrial plants are removing CO2 from the atmosphere through photosynthesis. During the fall and winter, plants are less active and atmospheric CO2 increases.
