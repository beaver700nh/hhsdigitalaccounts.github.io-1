OBJECTIVE: to discover how ATP stores energy

Your cells use the energy from food to contract, move, grow, send messages, and respond to their environment. However, cells cannot directly use the energy stored in food—they must change it into a more usable form, stored in a molecule called ATP (adenosine triphosphate).
