OBJECTIVE: to distinguish between producers and consumers

Producers are photosynthetic organisms such as plants, algae, and some bacteria. During photosynthesis, producers use the energy in sunlight to make food (complex organic molecules) from water, carbon dioxide, and minerals. In contrast, animals (including you!) are consumers. Consumers cannot use the energy in sunlight to make food. Instead, consumers must obtain food by eating producers or other consumers, or products made by a consumer.
