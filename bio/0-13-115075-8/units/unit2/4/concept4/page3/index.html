OBJECTIVE: to measure the pH of a solution using a simulated pH probe

You have learned that rainwater can be acidic. What does that mean? An aqueous solution, such as rain, contains both H+ and OH– ions. An acidic solution contains more H+ than OH– ions. A basic solution contains more OH– ions than H+ ions. A neutral solution contains equal concentrations of H+ and OH– ions. The pH scale measures the concentration of H+ compared to OH–. A pH of 7 is neutral. A pH of less than 7 is acidic, and a pH of more than 7 is basic.
