Life depends on the unique properties of water.


Investigate the structure and properties of water.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate the role of biological buffers through a simulated experiment

In this activity you will first see what happens to the pH of water as you add either acid or base. Then you will investigate what happens when you add acid or base to a solution that contains a biological buffer, such as ground-up cow's liver. Note that ground-up liver is just one example of a buffer; you also have buffers in your body. When you have finished this activity, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What happened when you added acid to the water? Base to the water?

2. What happened when you added acid to the liver? Base to the liver?

3. Can you suggest a reason that the liver solution behaved differently from the water solution after the addition of acid and base?

4. Death may occur if the pH of human blood goes above 7.55 or below 7.35. How can the pH of blood be maintained within these narrow limits?
