Prelab Activity
In 1995, storm clouds brewed over Shenandoah National Park in Virginia. Torrential rains fell, and in just three days, as much as 61 centimeters (24 inches) of rain soaked the area. Streams overflowed, and trees were uprooted. Huge amounts of debris washed downstream, scouring the streambeds and killing many trout. In many stream areas, the trout were completely eliminated.

Flood-damaged stream

When it was all over, fisheries biologists assessed the damage. They asked themselves the question: "Will the trout population be able to recover on its own, or will we need to intervene by introducing new trout to the area?"

In this activity, you will take on the role of a fisheries biologist. You will "go out" into a flood-affected stream to estimate the stream's trout population in 1997, two years after the flood.
