Although the Galápagos Islands are now a National Park and the present-day species are protected, there are many species that became extinct, or were on the brink of extinction, including several subspecies of the famous Galápagos tortoises (Geochelone elephantopus.) In 1971, scientists discovered the last surviving member of the subspecies Geochelone elephantopus abingdoni living on Pinta Island. He was nicknamed "Lonesome George."
