Closer Look: Circulation Cells

OBJECTIVE: to learn the effect of circulation cells in the atmosphere

Temperature and precipitation are the two main factors that determine the climate in a region. The uneven heating of Earth's surface and the way air circulates, or "circulation cells," over the surface cause much of the varied temperature and precipitation patterns on Earth. In this activity, you will study how the direction of air flow and the rise and fall of air masses in the circulation cells determine the amount of heat and water that are delivered to specific areas on Earth.
