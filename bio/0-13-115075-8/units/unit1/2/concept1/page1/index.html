Discovery science emphasizes inquiry and observation.


Observe and infer.

OBJECTIVE: to explore the way scientists use both observations and inferences in scientific studies

In this activity, you will test your observation skills and distinguish between observations and inferences. When you have finished the activity, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What is the difference between an observation and an inference? Give an example based on the elephant activity.

2. Describe the similarities and differences in the courtship behaviors between the two bird species.

3. Were any of the "observations" you made really inferences? Explain.

4. List four inferences you could make about courtship behaviors of birds based on your observations of the waved albatross and the blue-footed booby.

When you have completed the questions, go to the next activity.
