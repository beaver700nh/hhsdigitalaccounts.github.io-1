Introduction
To start your investigation, you will explore the tunnels and chambers inside a termite mound. You will see examples of the types, or castes, of termites found in a typical colony. You will also learn how each termite caste is adapted to perform specific jobs in the colony.

Background
Termites are insects that build large colonies either underground (subterranean termites) or inside wood (drywood termites). Most termites eat dead wood, although some species feed on living trees. Termites are able to digest certain plant fibers found in wood with the help of protists that live in the digestive systems of termites. The protists produce a chemical that breaks down plant fibers into compounds the termites can absorb.
