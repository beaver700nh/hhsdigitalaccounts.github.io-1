OBJECTIVE: to explore the diversity of a Costa Rican tropical rain forest

Rain forests currently cover less than 7 percent of Earth's land surface, but they are home to over 50 percent of all known species of living organisms. The tropical rain forest in the lowlands of Costa Rica is one of the most diverse in the world. As you examine the many different organisms in the Costa Rican rainforest scene below, think about the different organisms in the ecosystem and how they interact with one another.
