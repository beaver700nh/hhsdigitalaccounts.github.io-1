Biology explores life from the global to the microscopic scale.


Activate a metric scale bar.

OBJECTIVE: to compare sizes and scale using an interactive metric scale

The study of biology stretches from the global scale of the entire Earth down to the microscopic level of cells and molecules. Explore these levels of organization by navigating from space into Earth's biosphere, and down to the cellular level of an individual organism as you compare sizes and scale using powers of ten and the metric scale. After you investigate what you can see at different scales, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. As you zoom in from Earth to DNA, at what point on the measurement scale did you first need the help of an instrument, such as a microscope, to see what is in the photographs?

2. Is the diameter of an iguana cell larger or smaller than the width of a DNA molecule?

3. What metric unit, the centimeter or meter, would be most useful for measuring an object like an automobile? What unit, the meter or kilometer, would be most useful for measuring your high school campus, or grounds? What unit would be most useful for measuring the nucleus of a cell?

When you have completed the questions, go to the next activity.
