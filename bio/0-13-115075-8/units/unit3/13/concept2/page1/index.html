Biologists can engineer microbes to make useful products.


Cut and paste DNA.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate how restriction enzymes selectively "cut and paste" DNA

Restriction enzymes, produced by bacteria, will attach to specific DNA nucleotide sequences and cut the DNA at those sites. In this activity, you will cut a particular gene from a donor cell's DNA and insert it into a plasmid. When you are finished, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What is the role of the restriction enzyme?

2. Why do scientists keep a set of restriction enzymes?

3. Why might scientists want to insert a new gene into bacteria?

4. What is the role of the DNA ligase?

When you have completed the questions, go to the next activity.
