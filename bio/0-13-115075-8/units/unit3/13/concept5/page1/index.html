OBJECTIVE: to analyze a lac operon

Bacteria and other organisms usually make proteins only when they are needed. For example, E. coli live in your intestine and use your food as an energy source. When lactose (milk sugar) is abundant, and glucose (blood sugar) is not, E. coli will "turn on" the genes necessary to make the proteins that absorb and digest lactose. In this activity, you will investigate this process. The animation below shows a lac operon, which is a particular stretch of DNA (or a cluster of genes). The name "lac" is short for "lactose."
