Interpret a pedigree.

OBJECTIVE: to analyze a pedigree of a sex-linked trait

Another Mendelian pattern that occurs in humans involves sex-linked traits. Sex-linked traits are found on one sex chromosome (usually the X chromosome). Therefore, a male (XY) needs only one copy of a recessive X-linked allele to exhibit the recessive trait. Some sex-linked traits cause serious disorders. In this activity, you will analyze the pedigree of a European royal family, learn about a sex-linked condition called hemophilia, and determine the genotypes for various members of the family.
