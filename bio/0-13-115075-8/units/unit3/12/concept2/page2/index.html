Accidents affecting chromosomes can cause disorders.


Discover effects of chromosome changes.

OBJECTIVE: to identify how certain changes in chromosome structure may lead to disorders in humans

If mistakes in DNA replication and repair occur as gametes develop, these mistakes can be passed on to an embryo. There are four types of gene-level chromosomal changes: duplication of genes, deletion of genes, inversion of genes, and translocation of genes. In this activity, you will explore the possible consequences of these chromosomal changes. When you finish the activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Describe the difference between an inversion and a translocation.

2. Why is it usually a lethal situation when a deletion occurs in a gamete's chromosome?

3. Abnormal chromosomes in gametes (egg and sperm) that are involved in conception are potentially more damaging to the next generation than a chromosome abnormality that develops in an adult's skin cell. Explain why this is true.
