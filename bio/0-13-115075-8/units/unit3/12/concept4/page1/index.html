Genetic changes contribute to cancer.


Explore the DNA-cancer connection.

OBJECTIVE: to explore how changes in genes affect the proper functioning of the cell cycle

The cell cycle is controlled by genes that produce proteins (growth factors) that stimulate the passage of cells through the different stages of the cell cycle. Other genes (tumor-suppressor genes) normally stop the cells from leaving G1 and G2. In this activity, you will review the normal events in the cell cycle and explore what happens when the genes that control the cell cycle malfunction.
