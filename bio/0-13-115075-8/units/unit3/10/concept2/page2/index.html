Mendel discovered that inheritance follows rules of chance.



Apply Mendel's rules.

OBJECTIVE: to perform monohybrid (F1 x F1) crosses on the offspring of the cross between two different breeding parents, and discover what happens to recessive traits

When Mendel crossed true-breeding peas he found that the traits did not blend in the F1 generation; only the dominant trait was expressed. Mendel crossed individuals from the F1 generation. Like Mendel, you will perform F1 crosses in this activity and discover what happens to the expression of the parental traits. When you finish, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. If the "blending hypothesis of inheritance" was true, what offspring would you expect to find in the F2 generation?

2. If Mendel counted 232 offspring from the F1 x F1 cross between pea plants for flower color, about how many do you predict would be white? Purple?
