Meiosis explains Mendel's principles.


Examine a crossover event.

OBJECTIVE: to understand why genes located near each other on a chromosome are an exception to Mendel's principles of independent assortment and segregation; to observe that crossing over in meiosis may generate new combinations of alleles (recombination)

In the activity below, study what happens to genes on the same chromosomes, or linked genes, in Prophase I and Metaphase I and to each allele of the genes in Anaphase I. Be sure to scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete the answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Do genes B and C assort independently? Explain.

2. Do genes A and C assort independently? Explain.

3. In metaphase I, how often do the "A" and "B" alleles line up so that they move together in anaphase I?
