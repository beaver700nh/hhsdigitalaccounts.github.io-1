Meiosis increases genetic variation among offspring.


Analyze the process of crossing over.

OBJECTIVE: to observe how the process of crossing over increases genetic variation

The animation in this activity models how new combinations of genes are formed when two homologous chromosomes exchange pieces during meiosis. You will observe how this process, called crossing over, increases genetic variation. When you have completed the activity, scroll down to answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. One chromosome started with the genes ABCDEFG. What is the new combination of genes that resulted from crossing over?

2. One chromosome started with the genes abcdefgh. What is the new combination of genes that resulted from crossing over?

3. Suppose the crossing over occurred between E and f. What is the new combination of the two chromatids?
