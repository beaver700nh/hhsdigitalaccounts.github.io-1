In the lab procedure, you will prepare microscope slides of cells from onion root tips. The photos below show some of the steps you will perform in the lab. If you'd like a closer look at any ofthe photos, click on the image. A new browser window will open. Be sure to close the new browser window when you are finished.

Using the razor blade, you will carefully cut off the upper part of each root so that only about 3-4 mm of the tip end is left on the slide.



To make cell structures easier to see, you will stain each root tip with aceto-orcein stain.




You will place a cover slip on top the stained root tips and press very gently with the side of your forceps to squish the root tip completely flat.




You will then view your slide under the microscope. Move the slide slowly as needed to scan all the root tip cells and make your observations.
