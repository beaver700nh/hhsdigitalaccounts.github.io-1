OBJECTIVE: to examine how a fungus disperses its spores

Pilobolus is a fungus that decomposes dung piles. Dung piles are widely spread in the environment. How does a Pilobolus spore get to a dung pile in the first place? The microscopic spores of many fungi are dispersed by wind currents. Pilobolus is an exception. Its spores are dispersed with the dung when herbivores consume the spores, which are "packaged" in spore capsules called sporangia. In this activity, you will study the video of this process.
