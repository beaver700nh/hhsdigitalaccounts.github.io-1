Stop bacteria from dividing.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate how the antibiotic penicillin stops bacterial growth

Penicillin is a naturally occurring antibiotic that is produced by a common mold called Penicillium notatum. It stops the division of bacteria by interfering with the bacteria's ability to synthesize a cell wall. In this activity, you will grow bacteria on culture media (artificial nutrient material that is used to support the growth of cells or bacteria) both with and without penicillin and observe how penicillin impacts the growth of the bacterial cells. A culture medium is a nutrient material prepared for the growth of microorganisms in the laboratory. When you have finished, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Penicillin is a naturally produced antibiotic of the mold Penicillium. Why do you think the mold produces this antibiotic?

2. Penicillin is not effective in killing existing bacterial cells, but does prevent bacteria from reproducing. Explain.

3. Some bacteria can produce an enzyme called penicillinase that actually breaks down penicillin. What advantage does this give these types of bacteria?
