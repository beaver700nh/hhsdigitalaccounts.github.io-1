OBJECTIVE: to model a classic experiment that investigated how some of the organic molecules required for life could have arisen on early Earth

Fossil evidence indicates that life existed on Earth about 3.5 billion years ago. In this activity, you will virtually model a classic experiment, devised by Stanley Miller in 1953, to test the hypothesis that the organic compounds essential for life could be generated under early Earth conditions. When you have completed the activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. The early Earth environment had many different sources of energy available that helped drive chemical reactions. Name two sources of energy in this environment.

2. The early atmosphere contained many gases that our current atmosphere contains. Name one gas that was probably not present on early Earth.

3. Chemicals can be classified as organic or inorganic. Name at least two organic chemicals that resulted from Miller's experiment.
