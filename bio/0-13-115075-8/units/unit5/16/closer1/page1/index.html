Bacterial Pathogens

OBJECTIVE: to recognize the action of exotoxins and endotoxins produced by pathogenic bacteria

Bacterial pathogens cause harm by producing either exotoxins or endotoxins. Exotoxins are proteins released by a bacterium that damages a host's tissues. Endotoxins are components found on the outer cell wall of some Gram-negative bacteria. In this activity, you will study the mechanisms of exotoxins and endotoxins. Then you will investigate a few bacterial pathogens and determine whether each attacks the host with exotoxins or endotoxins. When you have finished, scroll down and answer the questions below.
