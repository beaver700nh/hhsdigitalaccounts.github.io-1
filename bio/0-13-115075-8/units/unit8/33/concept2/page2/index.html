Hormones regulate the reproductive systems.


Analyze the female reproductive cycles.

OBJECTIVE: to explore how fluctuating levels of pituitary and ovarian hormones regulate the ovarian and menstrual cycles in women

Hormones regulate and coordinate the ovarian and menstrual cycles. In this activity, you will investigate the fluctuation of these hormones and how they regulate the growth of the follicle and ovulation, and prepare the endometrium for possible implantation of the embryo. You will first examine pituitary hormones, which are released from the pituitary gland in the brain. When you have finished this activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What gland secretes FSH and LH into the blood?

2. Progesterone is one of the hormones that prepare the endometrium for implantation of the fertilized egg. A blood test can measure levels of hormones circulating in the blood. What do you predict would happen to the blood progesterone levels if fertilization and pregnancy occur?

3. If pregnancy does occur, what ovarian structure do you predict would remain intact during early pregnancy and secrete the hormones necessary for maintaining the endometrium?
