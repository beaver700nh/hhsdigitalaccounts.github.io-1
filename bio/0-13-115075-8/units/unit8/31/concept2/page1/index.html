OBJECTIVE: to examine the defenders involved in the nonspecific and specific body defenses

The human body has three lines of defense against infection. The first line consists of physical and chemical barriers that prevent pathogens from entering. The second line consists of chemicals and specialized cells that can destroy or disable pathogens that penetrate the barrier. The third line targets specific pathogens.
