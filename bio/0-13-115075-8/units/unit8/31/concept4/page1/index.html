Vaccines stimulate the immune response.


Vaccinate a patient.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate how vaccines provide immunity

Many people get flu shots to protect themselves against influenza virus infections. The flu shot, or vaccine, consists of a specific inactivated flu virus, so it cannot infect body cells. However, antigens still exist and will stimulate the body’s immune response. In this activity, you will explore what happens in the body after vaccination against a recent strain of influenza.
