Lab Procedure Photos
All photos were taken by Diane Sweeney with the permission of the scientists at IGeneX Inc, a diagnostic lab and research facility that specializes in testing for tick-borne diseases.

Special thanks to: Dr. Nick Harris and Dr. Shaw, who were particularly helpful in explaining the various methods used in Lyme disease testing.

ELISA Plate Reader
This photo shows a spectrophotometer that reads the absorbance of a certain wavelength of light through each sample on a 96-well plate. In the case of the ELISA assay, it will give quantitative data on how "blue" each positive well is. This can help technicians determine which samples may need to be retested.

ELISA Setup
This photo was taken at the lab bench of a technician who does Lyme Disease ELISA testing. These are the actual reagents used in the ELISA test. IGeneX, will do ELISA tests for Lyme disease only if another, more reliable, test is ordered at the same time. (The ELISA test for Lyme disease antigen is notorious for both false positive and false negative results).

Receiving Samples
This photo shows the shipping and receiving door at IgeneX. Shipping company employees hand off boxes of patient samples to gloved and masked technicians who would then open the boxes and handle the blood and urine samples to be tested.

IgG Western Blot
This photo shows the results of Western Blot tests using IgG from various patients suspected of having Lyme disease. The lanes on the left are the positive controls. The other lanes are patient samples. If a patient has 5 confirming bands, they are positive for Lyme disease.

IgM Western Blot
This photo shows the same test as the previous photo, using IgM antibody.

Loading Thermocycler for PCR
This photo shows a technician loading the thermocycler with urine or blood samples from patients. The thermocycler amplifies any DNA that specific B. burgdorferi primers will bind to.

Preparing DNA for the Thermocycler
This photo shows the DNA being concentrated and purified before PCR.
