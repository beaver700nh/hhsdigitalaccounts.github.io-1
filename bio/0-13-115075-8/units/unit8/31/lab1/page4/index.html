Prelab Questions
Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

When you finish the questions, be sure to check out the lab equipment that is used in a lab for a real ELISA test.

1. If antibodies bind to the Lyme disease antigen in the ELISA plate well, how do you know that the antibodies were released in response to a B. burgdorferi infection?

2. Would either the negative or positive control solution, or both control solutions, contain the primary antibody? Explain.

3. Suppose an ELISA test of a patient who had been exposed to B. burgdorferi several weeks before produced a negative result. Which of the following could be a possible explanation? Explain your response.

a. There was no primary antibody in the serum.
b. You forgot to add the substrate.
c. You did not change pipettes between samples.
d. You didn't allow enough time for the antigen to bind to the well.
e. b or d could have caused the problem.

4. When performing ELISA tests in a medical diagnostic lab, it is important to change pipettes between patient samples. Why?
