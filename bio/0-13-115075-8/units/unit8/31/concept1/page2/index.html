Infectious diseases are caused by pathogens.


Investigate how diseases spread.

OBJECTIVE: to determine how an infectious disease can spread rapidly and affect thousands of people

In 1918, World War I was coming to an end in Europe. A serious flu epidemic was also spreading. This deadly flu mainly affected people between the ages of 20 and 40. In the United States, the flu killed over 600,000 people, which was ten times more than the number of military deaths in World War I. Many of the soldiers returning to the United States were infected with the flu. But in 1918, the United States did not have a sufficient network of public health departments, as it does today, to communicate proper information about the spreading virus.
