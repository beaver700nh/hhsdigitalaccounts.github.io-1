&answer1= <b>The suggested answer is:</b> When a person has high blood pressure, proteins are sometimes forced from the blood into the tubule. Proteins are too large to be reabsorbed back into the blood from the tubule and eventually will leave the body in the urine.
&answer2= <b>The suggested answer is:</b> If blood glucose levels are very high, some of the glucose is not reabsorbed. Instead, the excess glucose remains in the nephron tubule and eventually leaves the body in the urine.
&answer3= <b>The suggested answer is:</b> There are a number of reasons why protein may be present in the urine (for example, kidney damage). Also, high blood pressure may not lead to protein in the urine.  By conducting the two tests, the doctor can rule out other problems and properly diagnose the patient.
