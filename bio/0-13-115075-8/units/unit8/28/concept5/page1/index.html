Sensory receptors link the environment to the nervous system.


Get an inside view of vision.

OBJECTIVE: to explore the structure of the retina and how light is perceived by the brain

Light rays entering the eye form an image on the retina. This image is converted into a pattern of nerve signals. In this activity, you will investigate how the photoreceptor cells in the retina (the rods and cones), detect color and light and then send this information to the brain. When you finish the activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.


Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. What does the lens do to the light rays?

2. Rods and cones have different functions in vision. What is the function of the cones? What is the function of the rods?

3. Trace the series of events that lead up to an image being interpreted in the occipital lobe of the brain once the light hits the retina.
