Get an inside view of vision.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate how a lens focuses light on the retina to create a clear image and how the shape of the eyeball may change the focal point

In order for you to see a clear image, the lens in your eye must bend and focus light onto your retina. In this activity, you will examine how a normally-shaped eye's lens changes shape in order to view objects close up and at a distance. Then you will explore two common vision problems and figure out how to correct them. When you finish the activity, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. The flexible lens is the key to clear vision. Describe the change in shape in a lens from when you are reading a book to looking off in the distance.

2. As people age, their lenses become more rigid. What problems might this present?

3. People who have abnormally shaped eyeballs usually have normally functioning lenses. Why do they need corrective glasses?

4. If you were an ophthalmologist (a doctor who specializes in the health care of the eye), what kind of lens would you recommend for someone who is nearsighted? Explain.
