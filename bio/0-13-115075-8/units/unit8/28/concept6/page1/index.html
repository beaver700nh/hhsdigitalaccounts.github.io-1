Certain drugs alter brain function.


Analyze the effects of drugs.

OBJECTIVE: to examine how cocaine disrupts normal activity at the synapse

Most drugs act by interfering with normal neurotransmitter functioning. For example, the illegal drug cocaine blocks the removal of the neurotransmitter dopamine from the synaptic cleft. Dopamine occurs naturally in the body and associated with pleasure and well-being in the limbic system of the brain.
