Neurons conduct nerve impulses.


Examine how neurons function.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate the action potential

The neuron at rest has a voltage of –70 mV across its plasma membrane. A change in this voltage is the energy source used to transmit a nerve signal. In this activity, you will explore how a nerve signal (a series of action potentials) is transmitted along a neuron. When you are finished, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. Describe the overall change in charge during the first step of an action potential.

2. Once an action potential has passed through an area of the membrane, what happens to the charge inside the cell?

3. How does an action potential sequence resemble a whole row of closely placed dominos that are tipped over? Explain.
