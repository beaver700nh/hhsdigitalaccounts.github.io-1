&answer1=<b>The correct answer is:</b> Mammals can be placental, marsupial, or egg layers. In placental mammals, the embryo receives its nutrition through the placenta, which is attached to the mother's uterine wall. In marsupials, the young are born very early in development and complete their early development attached to the mother's mammary gland within an external pouch. Egg-laying mammals lay eggs.
