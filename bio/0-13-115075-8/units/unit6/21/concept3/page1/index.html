Some plants have unique adaptations for nutrition.


Trigger a Venus' flytrap.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate how the Venus' flytrap captures its prey

Some plants living in nitrogen-poor environments obtain nitrogen by capturing and digesting insects. In the case of the Venus' flytrap, the capture mechanism requires energy, so the plant's ability to prevent the trap from springing unproductively, wasting energy, is an important adaptation. In this activity, you will investigate aspects of the capture mechanism. When you finish, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

2. The Venus' flytrap needs two hits on the trigger hairs to spring the trap. Why is this an advantage to the plant?
