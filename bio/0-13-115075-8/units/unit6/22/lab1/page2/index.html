Introduction
Plant hormones cause chemical changes in certain cells in response to environmental factors such as light, wind, temperature, touch, and gravity. In the Prelab Activity, you will observe some of the effects of gravity as you examine photographs of young radish seedlings grown on a water-soaked paper towel in an upright petri dish. Note that the "leaves" you will observe are the cotyledons or "seed leaves" as the photographs were taken on Days 1 through 3 after germination. In the lab, you will develop and test your own hypotheses about the effects of gravity on young plant stems. You will perform an experiment of your own design using plastic film canisters and seedlings.
