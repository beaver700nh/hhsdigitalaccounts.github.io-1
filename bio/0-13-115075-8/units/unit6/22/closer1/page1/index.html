Closer Look: Phytochromes

OBJECTIVE: to explore how phytochrome controls many plant processes

When experiments showed that flowering plants respond to the length of the darkness period and not the light, scientists searched for a chemical mechanism in the plant responsible for measuring the length of darkness. Eventually scientists discovered a pigment molecule called phytochrome that plays a role in floral photoperiodism as well as many other plant developmental processes. In this activity, you will examine the properties of phytochrome and how it directs the plant's response to light and dark. Below, you see an iris plant with a variable controller for hours of light and dark. In activity 22.3, you learned that the iris is a long-day (short night) plant and will not flower if nights are longer than ten hours.
