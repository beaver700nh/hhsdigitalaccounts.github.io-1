OBJECTIVE: to investigate the role of day length in plant flowering

For many plants, day length is a critical factor in the start of flower bud development. Some plants require a day length longer than some minimum amount of time (long-day plants), while other plants need a light period shorter than some maximum amount of time (short-day plants). Then there are plants that will flower at any day length as long as the plant has reached maturity (day-neutral plants). In this activity, you will investigate various plants to determine whether each is a day-neutral, long-day (short night), or short-day (long night) plant. When you finish, scroll down and answer the questions below.

Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. At the equator (day length is always 12 hours), would you expect to find long-day, short-day, or day-neutral plants?

2. A plant grower needs to prepare 500 flowering chrysanthemums (short-day plants with a critical day length of 14 hours) for sale in the early fall. What advice would you give the grower?

3. The Japanese morning glory is a short-day plant that flowers if the day length is less than 14 hours. A scientist places these plants in a growth chamber with alternating periods of 8 hours of light and 8 hours of dark. Will these plants flower? Explain.
