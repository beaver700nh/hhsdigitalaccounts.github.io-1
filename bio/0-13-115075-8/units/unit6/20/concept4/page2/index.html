Secondary growth increases the thickness of woody plants.


Examine secondary growth.

OBJECTIVE: to investigate the production of secondary xylem and phloem by the vascular cambium

Woody plants increase their width through a process known as secondary growth. The vascular cambium is a cylinder of actively dividing cells located between the xylem and the phloem. In this activity, you will discover how the vascular cambium creates secondary xylem tissue (wood) and secondary phloem tissue through cell division.
