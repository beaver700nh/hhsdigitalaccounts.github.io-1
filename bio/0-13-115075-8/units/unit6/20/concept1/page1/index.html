OBJECTIVE: to label the parts of a flower and identify flower structures in photographs

The four types of modified leaves of a flower (sepal, petal, stamen, and carpel) vary greatly in size, number, and shape. After you identify these parts in a diagram of a flower, you will identify these structures in photographs of real flowers. When you are finished, scroll down and answer the questions below.


Answer the following questions in the boxes provided. After you complete each answer, click the Check Your Answer button for feedback.

1. In many flowers, such as the tulip shown in the middle photograph, the sepals look identical to the petals. How might you be able to tell which structures are petals and which are sepals? (Hint: Think about how these flower parts are organized.)

2. The carpels are composed of what three parts?

3. In plants that have petals, the petals tend to be large, colorful structures. What is the function of these petals?

4. After fertilization, the apple blossom ovary develops into the apple core. What do you think the ovule develops into?
