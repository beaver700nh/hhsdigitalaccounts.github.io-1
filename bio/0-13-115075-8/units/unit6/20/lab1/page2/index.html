Introduction
To start your investigation, you will explore how the features of flowers can attract specific animal pollinators. During the investigation you will "think like a botanist" as you dissect flowers and examine the parts that are involved in pollination and the production of seeds.

Background
Flowers, which are unique to angiosperms, function in reproduction. The flowers of many angiosperms have adapted in ways that attract pollinators, including animals such as birds or insects that carry pollen between flowers. For example, the flower features that attract hummingbirds and bees are described in the table below. As a bird or insect feeds on a flower's nectar or pollen, the pollen sticks to the animal. When the pollinator moves on to another plant of the same species, it pollinates the second plant. Pollination may lead to fertilization and the development of a new generation of plants.

Flower Feature Hummingbirds Bees
Shape Hummingbirds prefer flowers with a tubular shape that fit their long, slender beaks. Bees prefer cup-shaped flowers in which they can nestle while gathering pollen or nectar.
Color Hummingbirds as well as many other birds, are most attracted to red flowers, but will also drink nectar from flowers with colors that contain red such as orange or pink. Bees, as well as other insects, respond mostly to blue and yellow flowers. They cannot detect the color red.
Pattern Hummingbirds respond to color and shape, rather than patterns. Bees are attracted to flowers with "runway" lines or dashes that lead to the bee to pollen or nectar.
Scent Hummingbirds have a very limited sense of smell. Smell has little impact on their food choice. Bees have a strong sense of smell and are attracted to fragrant flowers.
