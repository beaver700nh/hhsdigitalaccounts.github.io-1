Primary growth lengthens roots and shoots.


Dissect a root.

OBJECTIVE: to observe the primary growth of a root and explore its internal structure

In roots, primary growth (growth in length) occurs in three stages: 1) cell division proceeds in the root's tip in a tissue called a meristem; 2) some of the new cells elongate; and then 3) the elongated cells develop into the dermal, ground, and vascular tissues.
